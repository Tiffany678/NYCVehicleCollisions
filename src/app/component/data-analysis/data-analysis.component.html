What Causes Car Accidents in New York City?

Car accidents happen every day in New York City. These collisions and crashes happen for many different reasons. However, certain things tend to factor into the equation more often than others. According to the NYPD, the three leading causes of traffic accidents in the analyzed 1000 records are:

  • Driver Inattention / Distraction (22.5%)
  • Failure to Yield Right-of-way (7.1%)
  • Unsafe Speed (4.9%)

Accidents Resulting in an Injury and Killed

According to the open source, most collision won't cause injury or death which is about 67.6% chance. There are about 2.3% chance of having one person injured. For 1000 car incidents, there are 16 people injured and 2 people dead.

When Do Most NYC Car Accidents Happen?

There are often some hours in a day which we observee more accidents than other times in a day. According to the NYPD's crash data, 5 PM is the time with the highest number of reported accidents (69 cases), followed closely by 6 PM (60 cases). This makes logical senses that these are the rush hours with more cars on the roads.

Traffic Accidents Occured the Most in which NYC Borough?

Which borough is the most dangerous for drivers? Brooklyn. In these 1000 cases, roughly one-fourth (22.3 %) of all NYC accidents happened there. Queens, Manhattan, and the Bronx are also quite dangerous. Those boroughs account for 17.4%, 11.1%, and 8.9% of accidents, respectively. Staten Island reports the fewest number of accidents. This borough sees less than 6 percent of the city's collisions.
